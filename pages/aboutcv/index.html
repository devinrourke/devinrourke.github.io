About/CV

Hi, I'm Devin. I'm a runner, coach, and PhD student living in Boulder, CO.

What this blog isn't.

Nothing here is related to my PhD research, but I'll briefly summarize my research path: I work in semiconductor materials fabrication and characterization. More specifically, throughout my undergraduate work at CU Boulder and for two years afterward, I worked at NIST Boulder making cool electronic devices out of nanoscale materials (BioFETs utilizing GaN nanowires, for you nerds). Because of the purity and sensitivity required to make and test such devices, I often donned a bunny suit and worked in a cleanroom (pictured right).
      When I grew out of my bunny suit, I traded it in for 6(?) more years of school, this time in Physics (MS) and EE (eventual PhD). Lately, I have switched my focus to the computer modeling and simulation of materials. I'm interested in how we can use computers to imagine, design, and predict the nanoscale behavior of useful materials. In the CU Boulder ECEE Department, and along with Dr. Won Park and our collaborators, I examine how light interacts with nanostructured materials. One area that my research has impacted is that of photovoltaics.       You can read more about some of my latest research here.       That's all I'll say about my research on this blog, but if you have questions or want to know more, just drop me an email!

What this blog is.

Everything on this blog is about my peripheral interests and projects. I enjoy learning new things and since writing things down helps me remember them, I'll do some of that here. The most-developed section of this blog is Projects, where I document some of the side projects I have worked on. At this point, most of my (side, personal-interest) projects are in the data science, visualization, or web development realms. The creation of this blog for example, was (and still is, ongoing) a project that I describe in this post. Another project I have spent a lot of time on is 'milesplit', where I have been analyzing trends in webscraped High School runner data. More about that project here.
An encouraging trend!

Another big part of my life is running. The Running section of this blog is where I put general thoughts about my training, or the occasional race report. As of the Summer/Fall of 2017, I'm continuing to train with friends in the Boulder Track Club in preparation for a return trip to the California International Marathon. I also regularly post my training on Strava, if you'd like to follow along.
That's me in the back.

Lastly, I have benefited greatly from some incredible mentors and teachers in my life, and I wish to have that kind of an impact on others. As a teacher and a coach I have enjoyed discovering the profound similarities between classroom STEM pedagogy and coaching young athletes. In the Teaching & Coaching section, I collect thoughts on effective practices and valuable lessons in the teaching and coaching professions. I have taught at the Elementary, Middle, High School, and College Level, at STEM summer schools; mathematics and science claassrooms; student teaching Pre-IB chemistry and physics; and algebra and calculus based physics, respectively. I coach the Centaurus High School Cross Country and Track teams, in Lafayette, Colorado.
