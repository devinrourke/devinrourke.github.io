About/CV

Hi, I'm Devin. I'm a runner, coach, and PhD student living in Boulder, CO.

This blog

I'm not much of a blogger, but I enjoy learning new things and since writing things down helps me remember them, I'll do some of that here. The most-developed section of this blog is Projects, where I document some of the side projects I have worked on. At this point, most of my (side, personal-interest) projects are in the data science, visualization, or web development realms. The creation of this blog for example, was a little project that I describe in this post.
An interesting trend.

Another big part of my life is running. The Running section of this blog is where I put general thoughts about my own training, or the occasional race report. As of the Summer/Fall of 2017, I'm continuing to train with friends in the Boulder Track Club in preparation for a return trip to the California International Marathon. I regularly post my training on Strava, if you'd like to follow along.
That's me in the back.

Lastly, I have benefited greatly from some incredible mentors and teachers in my life, and I wish to have that kind of an impact on others. As a teacher and a coach I have enjoyed discovering the profound similarities between classroom STEM pedagogy and coaching young athletes. In the Teaching & Coaching section, I collect thoughts on effective practices and valuable lessons in the teaching and coaching professions. I have taught at the Elementary, Middle, High School, and College Level, at STEM summer schools; mathematics and science claassrooms; student teaching Pre-IB chemistry and physics; and algebra and calculus based Physics I and II, respectively. I coach the Centaurus High School Cross Country and Centaurus Track teams, in Lafayette, Colorado.

As far as my traditional educational endeavors and career, I'm currently a 5th(?) year PhD student in the CU Boulder ECEE Department. With Dr. Won Park and our collaborators, I examine how light interacts with various materials in order to benefit from unique behavior at the nanoscale. One area that my research has impacted is that of photovoltaics. You can read more about some of my research here.
