About/Contact

Hi, I'm Devin. I'm a runner, coach, data nerd, engineer and PhD candidate living in Boulder, CO.

What this isn't

PhD research.

But, and only here, I'll summarize my academic path to date: Throughout my undergrad at CU Boulder and for two years afterward, I worked at NIST Boulder in semiconductor materials fabrication and characterization. I made cool electronic devices out of nanoscale materials (BioFETs utilizing GaN nanowires, for you nerds). Because of the purity of the fab, and sensitivity of characterization of these things, I often donned a bunny suit and worked in a cleanroom (pictured right). In school, I studied Engineering Physics and Applied Math, and traveled to Nepal multiple times with Engineers Without Borders.


Then I grew out of my bunny suit. I traded it in for 6 6.5 7 more years of school, this time in Physics (MS) and EE (eventual PhD). Lately, In the CU Boulder ECEE Department, and along with Dr. Won Park and our collaborators, I am building computer models to simulate how light interacts with really small stuff. Like, smaller than the wavelength of the light. In particular, you can make solar panels more efficient. Efficient and cheap solar panels will solve climate change. See how important?


You can read more about some of my latest research here. That's all I'll say about my research on this blog, but if you have questions or want to know more, just drop me an email!

What this is

A Sandbox.

I enjoy learning new things (like Pelican!, a static site generator written in Python) and since 1) doing things and 2) writing them down helps me remember them, I'll do some of that here. Projects is where I play in the sand with things like HTML5, CSS3, Python, pandas, matplotlib, Apache Spark.. all sorts of side projects. At this point, most of my (side, personal-interest) projects are in the data science, visualization, or web development realms. The creation of this blog for example, was (and still is, ongoing) a project that I describe in this post. Another project is 'milesplit', where I have been analyzing trends in webscraped High School runner data. More about that project here.
